About Me

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Physics from Sharif University of Technology, specializing in astrophysics, cosmology, and computational methods. My research focuses on understanding the early universe, gravitational waves, and large-scale cosmic structures. I develop computational tools using Python, C++, and Mathematica to solve complex astrophysical problems and contribute to observational and theoretical cosmology.

Current Project

Deep Dive into Weinberg's Cosmology
Currently, I am working on an individual study of Weinberg's *Cosmology* with Dr. Ali Akbar Abolhasani. This project focuses on exploring the early universe, revisiting key theories, and refining our understanding of cosmological phenomena. Through hands-on derivations and numerical computations, I aim to build a solid theoretical framework for studying the cosmos.

Previous Project

Python Development for Iran National Observatory
During the summer of 2024, I contributed to the Iran National Observatory under the guidance of Dr. Amin Farhang. My work involved developing Python algorithms and data analysis tools for astronomical observations. Inspired by the systems used at the European Southern Observatory (ESO), I implemented tools like a Sky Magnitude Calculator and Ephemeris Generator, helping optimize Iran's largest optical telescope, the INO340.
